What I Do

my Activities and Interests
Research
My research focuses on image processing for materials science. In my PhD, I investigated motion estimation for in situ material tests. Since recently, I focus on deducing materials laws from image based data. I have a diverse background in mathematics, ranging from inverse problems over partial differential equations and variational methods to differential geometry. As I always focused on applied mathematics, I have rich experience in implementing solution algorithms for manifold problems in mathematics.
My Papers
Development

Working with 3D images requires out-of-the-box solution algorithms. In my PhD, I mainly worked with Matlab and C++. Most of my experience in other programming languages stems from my rich experience as a working student.

Training
During my time as a PhD and PostDoc I was also involved in several teaching activites. I organized and held exercises in basic courses on stochastics, regression and time series analysis and on higher mathematics for engineers.
I have shared my research results on several occasions with various communities. I attended conferences on mathematics, computed tomography ad experimental mechanics. Some of my talks can be found here.
